# Philippine Tax Treaties and International Taxation

## Executive Summary

This report covers Philippine tax treaties, international taxation provisions, and the regulatory framework governing cross-border transactions. The Philippines has established tax treaties with **44 countries** and has implemented transfer pricing regulations aligned with OECD guidelines.

---

## 3. Tax Sparing Provisions

### Overview

The Philippine Tax Code allows reduction of the 25% dividend withholding tax to **15%** for non-resident foreign corporations (NRFCs).

### Conditions for Tax Sparing Eligibility

1. The country of domicile allows a credit against tax due for taxes deemed paid in the Philippines equivalent to at least **15%**
2. OR the country of domicile exempts from tax the dividends derived from the Philippines

### Required Documentation

- Authenticated/apostilled copy of the foreign country's law allowing tax credit
- Document from foreign tax authority showing deemed paid credit
- Application filed with ITAD within **90 days** from dividend remittance

## 4. Permanent Establishment (PE) Rules

### Definition

A **Permanent Establishment** is a fixed place of business in the Philippines where the business of a foreign enterprise is wholly or partly carried on.

### Types of PE in Philippine Treaties

**1. Fixed Place PE**
- Office, branch, or place of management
- Factory, workshop, warehouse
- Mine, quarry, or extraction site
- Building site or construction project (typically exceeding 6-12 months)

**2. Service PE**
Foreign corporations may have a PE if they furnish services through employees for **more than 183 days in a twelve-month period**.

**3. Agency PE**
- **Dependent agents** with authority to conclude contracts may create a PE
- **Independent agents** acting in ordinary course of business generally do not create a PE

### Tax Treatment of PEs

- A PE is still treated as a **Non-Resident Foreign Corporation (NRFC)** for income taxation
- Without a PE, business profits are generally exempt from Philippine taxation

## 5. Transfer Pricing Regulations

### Legal Framework

**Revenue Regulations No. 02-2013 (RR 02-2013)** established comprehensive transfer pricing guidelines based on **OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines**.

### Arm's Length Principle

Transactions with related parties must be made under comparable conditions and circumstances as transactions with independent parties.

### Accepted Transfer Pricing Methods

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **CUP** (Comparable Uncontrolled Price) | Compares prices in controlled vs. uncontrolled transactions |
| **Resale Price Method** | Applies margin earned by reseller |
| **Cost Plus Method** | Applies markup to costs incurred |
| **TNMM** (Transactional Net Margin Method) | Examines net profit margin |
| **Profit Split Method** | Divides combined profits |

### Documentation Requirements

**BIR Form No. 1709** (Related Party Transaction Form) must be filed by:
- Large Taxpayers
- Taxpayers with net operating losses for three consecutive years
- Related parties transacting with the above

**Retention Period**: 10 years

## 6. Thin Capitalization Rules

### Current Status

**The Philippines has NO formal thin capitalization rules.**

Unlike many jurisdictions with specific debt-to-equity ratios, the Philippines does not have statutory limits on interest deductions based on leverage.

### Interest Deductibility Requirements

Interest is deductible if:
1. The indebtedness is connected with taxpayer's trade or business
2. The interest must have been **stipulated in writing**

---

## 7. Controlled Foreign Corporation (CFC) Rules

### Current Status

**The Philippines has NO Controlled Foreign Corporation (CFC) regime.**

### Available Anti-Avoidance Measures

1. **Section 50 Authority**: Commissioner may distribute/apportion income between related organizations
2. **Transfer Pricing Rules**: Related party transactions must comply with arm's length principle
3. **General Anti-Avoidance Provisions**: BIR monitors tax avoidance schemes

## 8. BEPS Implementation in the Philippines

### BEPS Inclusive Framework Status

**The Philippines has NOT formally joined the OECD/G20 BEPS Inclusive Framework.**

### BEPS Multilateral Instrument (MLI)

**The Philippines has NOT signed the BEPS MLI.**

### Pillar Two (Global Minimum Tax) Implementation

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Formal Adoption | Not yet adopted |
| QDMTT Implementation | Under consideration |
| Current Position | "Wait and see" |

### CREATE MORE Act Alignment

The **CREATE MORE Act** aligns the Philippines with the OECD's **15% Global Minimum Tax** requirement.

## 9. Tax Treaty Relief Application (TTRA) Process

### Governing Regulations

- **RMO No. 14-2021**: Streamlined procedures
- **RMC No. 20-2022**: Clarifications on recurring transactions

### Two Application Types

**1. Request for Confirmation (RFC)**
- Filed when withholding agent applies preferential rate outright
- Deadline: Last day of 4th month after close of taxable year

**2. Tax Treaty Relief Application (TTRA)**
- Filed when withholding agent uses regular rate
- Non-resident seeks refund of excess tax withheld

### Documentary Requirements

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **BIR Form No. 0901** | Application for Treaty Purposes |
| **Tax Residency Certificate (TRC)** | Issued by foreign tax authority |
| **Applicable Treaty Provision** | Relevant treaty article |
| **Bank Documents** | Evidence of payment/remittance |
| **Special Power of Attorney** | Authorized representative |
| **Contract** | Executed by parties |

**Authentication**: Documents executed abroad must be either:
- Authenticated by Philippine Embassy, OR
- Apostilled (for Hague Convention signatories)

### Processing Timeline

- New TTRAs: **4 months** from submission of complete documents

### Outcomes

**Approval**: Certificate of Entitlement (COE) issued
**Denial**: BIR Ruling with explanation

## 10. Most-Favored Nation (MFN) Clauses

### Overview

Several Philippine tax treaties contain **MFN clauses** that may reduce withholding tax rates to the lowest rate granted to any third country.

### RP-US Tax Treaty MFN Clause

**Article 13(2)(b)(iii)** limits Philippine tax on royalties to the lowest rate imposed on similar royalties to any third country.

### Supreme Court Rulings on MFN

| Case | Ruling |
|------|--------|
| **CIR v. S.C. Johnson (1999)** | MFN requires royalties to be "of the same kind" and same double taxation mechanism |
| **Cargill Philippines (2020)** | Failed to show treaties grant "similar tax reliefs" |

### BIR Position

Following Supreme Court rulings:
- Applications invoking Czech or UAE treaties via MFN have been **denied**
- BIR requires proof of similar credit limitation mechanisms

## Additional Topics

### Exchange of Information (EOI)

**FATCA**: Philippines signed FATCA agreement with US (July 13, 2015)

**CRS**: Philippines has **not yet implemented** the Common Reporting Standard

### Branch Profit Remittance Tax (BPRT)

- Rate: **15%** on profits remitted by Philippine branch to head office
- May be reduced under applicable tax treaties
