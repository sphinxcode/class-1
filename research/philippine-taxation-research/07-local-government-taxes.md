# Philippine Local Government Taxation

## Executive Summary

This report covers the comprehensive framework of local government taxation in the Philippines as established by Republic Act No. 7160, otherwise known as the Local Government Code of 1991 (LGC). The LGC grants local government units (LGUs) the power to create their own sources of revenue.

---

## 1. Real Property Tax (RPT) - Rates and Assessment Levels

### Legal Basis
Real Property Tax is enshrined under Sections 232-283 of RA 7160. It is an ad valorem tax levied on real properties such as land, buildings, machinery, and other improvements.

### Basic Tax Rates (Section 233)

| Local Government Unit | Maximum Tax Rate |
|----------------------|------------------|
| Provinces | Up to **1%** of assessed value |
| Cities | Up to **2%** of assessed value |
| Municipalities within Metro Manila | Up to **2%** of assessed value |

### Assessment Levels (Section 218)

**For Lands:**

| Classification | Assessment Level |
|---------------|------------------|
| Residential | 20% |
| Agricultural | 40% |
| Commercial | 50% |
| Industrial | 50% |
| Mineral | 50% |
| Timberland | 20% |

**For Buildings and Structures:**

| Classification | Assessment Level Range |
|---------------|------------------------|
| Residential | 0% (under PHP 175,000) to 60% (over PHP 10 million) |
| Agricultural | 25% to 50% |
| Commercial | 30% to 80% |
| Industrial | 30% to 80% |

**For Machinery:**

| Classification | Assessment Level |
|---------------|------------------|
| Agricultural | 40% |
| Residential | 50% |
| Commercial/Industrial | 80% |

### Real Property Tax Exemptions (Section 234)

1. **Government-owned properties**
2. **Churches and religious institutions** (including parsonages, convents, mosques)
3. **Charitable and educational institutions**
4. **Cooperatives** duly registered under RA 6938
5. **Local water utilities** (machinery and equipment)
6. **Pollution control equipment**

### Payment and Penalties

- **Annual payment**: Due on or before January 31
- **Quarterly payment**: January 31, March 31, June 30, September 30
- **Early payment discount**: Up to 20% in some LGUs
- **Penalty for late payment**: 2% per month, maximum 72%

## 2. Local Business Tax Rates

### Legal Basis
Local Business Tax (LBT) is governed by Sections 143-154 of RA 7160.

### Municipal Business Tax Rates (Section 143)

**A. Manufacturers, Assemblers, Repackers, Processors:**

| Gross Sales/Receipts | Annual Tax |
|---------------------|------------|
| Under PHP 10,000 | PHP 165.00 |
| PHP 10,000 - 15,000 | PHP 275.00 |
| PHP 50,000 - 75,000 | PHP 1,320.00 |
| PHP 100,000 - 150,000 | PHP 2,200.00 |
| PHP 500,000 - 750,000 | PHP 8,000.00 |
| PHP 1,000,000 - 2,000,000 | PHP 13,750.00 |
| Over PHP 6,500,000 | Up to 37.5% of 1% |

**B. Retailers:**

| Gross Sales/Receipts | Maximum Tax Rate |
|---------------------|------------------|
| PHP 400,000 or less | 2% of gross sales |
| Over PHP 400,000 | 1% of gross sales |

**C. Contractors:**

| Gross Receipts | Annual Tax |
|---------------|------------|
| Under PHP 5,000 | PHP 27.50 |
| PHP 1,000,000 - 2,000,000 | PHP 11,500.00 |
| Over PHP 2,000,000 | Up to 50% of 1% |

**D. Banks/Financial Institutions:** 50% of 1% on lending income

**E. Essential Commodities Dealers:** Half (1/2) of applicable rates

### City Business Tax Rates (Section 151)

Cities may levy taxes that the province or municipality may impose. **Cities may exceed the maximum rates by not more than 50%**.

### Payment Schedule

- **Annual payment**: January 20
- **Quarterly payment**: January 20, April 20, July 20, October 20

## 3. Community Tax (Cedula)

### Legal Basis
Community tax is governed by Sections 156-161 of RA 7160.

### Rates for Individuals (Section 157)

| Component | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Basic Community Tax | PHP 5.00 (fixed) |
| Additional Tax | PHP 1.00 per PHP 1,000 of income |
| Maximum Additional Tax | PHP 5,000.00 |

**Who must pay:**
- Inhabitants 18 years or older regularly employed for at least 30 consecutive days
- Those engaged in business or occupation
- Owners of real property with assessed value of PHP 1,000 or more

### Rates for Corporations (Section 158)

| Component | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Basic Community Tax | PHP 500.00 (fixed) |
| Additional Tax (Real Property) | PHP 2.00 per PHP 5,000 of property value |
| Additional Tax (Gross Receipts) | PHP 2.00 per PHP 5,000 of gross receipts |
| Maximum Additional Tax | PHP 10,000.00 |

### Payment

- **Deadline**: Last day of February each year

## 4. Professional Tax

### Legal Basis
Professional tax is governed by Section 139 of RA 7160.

### Rate Structure

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Maximum Annual Fee | PHP 300.00 |
| Adjustment | Up to 10% every 5 years |
| Payment Deadline | January 31 annually |

### Key Provisions

- Applies to persons engaged in professions requiring government examination
- Payable to the province where profession is practiced
- Payment entitles practice anywhere in the Philippines
- **Multiple professions require separate payments**
- **Exemption**: Professionals exclusively employed in government

## 5. Franchise Tax at Local Level

### Legal Basis
Franchise tax is governed by Section 137 of RA 7160.

### Rate Structure

| Category | Maximum Rate |
|----------|-------------|
| Existing Businesses | 50% of 1% (0.5%) of gross annual receipts |
| Newly Started Businesses | 1/20 of 1% (0.05%) of capital investment |

### Key Features

- Imposed by provinces on businesses enjoying a franchise
- **Overrides any exemption granted by other laws**

---

## 6. Idle Land Tax

### Legal Basis
Idle land tax is governed by Section 236 of RA 7160.

### Rate Structure

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Maximum Rate | **5%** of assessed value |
| Nature | Additional to basic real property tax |
| Purpose | Discourage speculation; promote productive use |

### Who May Levy

- Provinces
- Cities
- Municipalities within Metropolitan Manila Area

---

## 7. Special Education Fund (SEF)

### Legal Basis
The SEF is governed by Section 235 (additional levy) and Section 272 (use of proceeds) of RA 7160.

### Rate

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Rate | **1%** of assessed value of real property |
| Nature | Additional levy on top of basic RPT |

### Distribution

- **Provinces**: Shared equally between province and component municipality
- **Cities**: Retained entirely by the city

### Allowable Uses (Section 272)

1. Operation and maintenance of public schools
2. Sports development
3. Construction, repair, and maintenance of school buildings
4. Establishment and maintenance of extension classes

## 8. Powers of LGUs under Local Government Code

### Fundamental Principles (Section 130)

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Uniformity | Taxation shall be uniform in each LGU |
| Equity | Based on taxpayer's ability to pay |
| Public Purpose | Levied only for public purposes |
| Reasonableness | Not unjust, excessive, or confiscatory |
| Legality | Not contrary to law or public policy |
| Exclusive Benefit | Revenue inures solely to the levying LGU |

### Provincial Taxes (Sections 134-140)

| Tax Type | Maximum Rate |
|----------|-------------|
| Transfer of Real Property | 50% of 1% of consideration/FMV |
| Printing and Publication | 50% of 1% of gross receipts |
| Franchise Tax | 50% of 1% of gross receipts |
| Sand, Gravel, Quarry Resources | 10% of FMV per cubic meter |
| Professional Tax | PHP 300.00 annually |
| Amusement Tax | 10% of gross admission receipts |

**Sand, Gravel Distribution:**
- Province: 30%
- Municipality/City: 30%
- Barangay: 40%

### Common Limitations on Taxing Powers (Section 133)

LGUs **cannot** levy taxes on:
1. **Income tax** (except on banks/financial institutions)
2. **Documentary stamp tax**
3. **Customs duties and taxes on imports/exports**
4. **Taxes on Philippine products actually exported**
5. **Cooperatives** (under RA 6938)
6. **National Government, its agencies, and other LGUs**
7. **BOI-registered pioneer enterprises** (6-year exemption)
8. **BOI-registered non-pioneer enterprises** (4-year exemption)
