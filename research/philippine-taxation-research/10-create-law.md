# Philippine CREATE Law (RA 11534)

## Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises Act

## Executive Summary

Republic Act No. 11534, known as the "Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises Act" or "CREATE," represents the second package of the Philippine Comprehensive Tax Reform Program. Signed into law on March 26, 2021, CREATE has been characterized as the largest fiscal stimulus for businesses in recent Philippine history, providing more than PHP 1 trillion worth of tax relief over a 10-year period.

---

## 1. Overview and Objectives of RA 11534

### Declaration of Policy

The law declares the State's policy to develop the national economy by:
- Attracting investments
- Enhancing productivity
- Generating employment
- Promoting countrywide development
- Achieving inclusive economic growth
- Maintaining fiscal prudence and stability

### Legislative History

- **Signing Date:** March 26, 2021
- **Publication:** March 27, 2021
- **Effective Date:** April 11, 2021

## 2. Corporate Income Tax Rate Reduction Schedule

### Standard Rate Reduction

| Period | CIT Rate | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Pre-CREATE | 30% | Highest in ASEAN |
| July 1, 2020 onwards | 25% | Retroactive application |
| 2023 | 24% | 1% annual reduction begins |
| 2024 | 23% | Gradual reduction continues |
| 2025 | 22% | Annual 1% decrease |
| 2026 | 21% | Continued reduction |
| 2027 onwards | 20% | Target final rate |

### Retroactive Application

The reduced 25% CIT rate applies **retroactively from July 1, 2020**, affecting 2020 corporate income tax filings.

### Minimum Corporate Income Tax (MCIT)

| Period | MCIT Rate |
|--------|-----------|
| Previous | 2% of gross income |
| July 1, 2020 to June 30, 2023 | 1% of gross income |
| Post-June 30, 2023 | 2% of gross income |

## 3. MSME Reduced Rates

### Preferential Rate for Small Corporations

MSMEs receive a **20% corporate income tax rate** (reduced from 30%).

### Qualifying Criteria

To qualify for the reduced 20% rate, corporations must meet BOTH conditions:
1. **Net Taxable Income:** Not exceeding PHP 5,000,000
2. **Total Assets:** Not exceeding PHP 100,000,000 (excluding land)

### Rate Comparison

| Corporation Type | Pre-CREATE Rate | CREATE Rate |
|-----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| MSMEs (qualifying) | 30% | 20% |
| Large Corporations | 30% | 25% |

## 4. Proprietary Educational Institutions and Hospitals Rates

### Preferential Tax Rate Structure

| Period | Tax Rate |
|--------|----------|
| July 1, 2020 - June 30, 2023 | 1% |
| July 1, 2023 onwards | 10% |

### Definition of Proprietary Educational Institution

A private school maintained and administered by private individuals or groups with an issued permit from:
- Department of Education (DepEd)
- Commission on Higher Education (CHED)
- Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA)

### Clarification via RA 11635

- **Educational Institutions:** The "nonprofit" qualification does NOT apply
- **Hospitals:** Must be nonprofit to qualify for preferential rates

### 50% Threshold Condition

If gross income from unrelated trade, business, or other activity exceeds 50% of total gross income, the entity becomes subject to the regular 25% CIT rate.

## 5. Rationalization of Fiscal Incentives

### Strategic Investment Priority Plan (SIPP)

Activities are classified into three tiers:

**Tier 1 Activities:**
- High potential for job creation and value creation
- Essential support to critical sectors
- Examples: Modern agriculture, food processing, healthcare, BPO

**Tier 2 Activities:**
- Manufacturing of supplies not produced domestically
- Import substitution and domestic value-chain development
- Examples: Electric vehicle assembly, renewable energy

**Tier 3 Activities:**
- Critical to structural transformation and industrial revolution
- Focus on innovation and R&D
- Examples: Advanced manufacturing, AI, innovation facilities

### Income Tax Incentives Structure

**For Export Enterprises:**
- Income Tax Holiday (ITH): 4-7 years
- Followed by: 5% SCIT OR Enhanced Deductions for 10 years

**For Domestic Market Enterprises (DMEs):**
- Income Tax Holiday (ITH): 4-7 years
- Followed by: Enhanced Deductions for 5-10 years
- Note: 5% SCIT is NOT available to DMEs

## 6. Fiscal Incentives Review Board (FIRB) Role

### Enhanced Powers Under CREATE

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Policy Making | Oversight on administration of tax incentives |
| Approval Authority | Approve/disapprove grants of tax incentives (>PHP 15B) |
| Strategic Planning | Formulate place-specific investment plans |
| Enforcement | Cancel, suspend, or withdraw incentives |
| Monitoring | Require submission of incentives data |
| Oversight | Regulatory authority over 13 IPAs |

### Investment Approval Thresholds (CREATE MORE)

| Investment Capital | Approval Authority |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| PHP 15 billion and below | Investment Promotion Agencies (IPAs) |
| More than PHP 15 billion | FIRB (upon IPA recommendation) |

## 7. Enhanced Deductions Regime (EDR)

### Corporate Income Tax Rate Under EDR

- **Original CREATE Rate:** 25% CIT on taxable income
- **CREATE MORE Rate:** 20% CIT on taxable income

### Additional Deductions Available

| Category | Deduction Rate |
|----------|----------------|
| Research and Development | 100% |
| Training Expenses | 100% |
| Power Expenses | 100% |
| Depreciation - Buildings | 10% |
| Depreciation - Machinery/Equipment | 20% |
| Labor Expenses | 50% |
| Domestic Input Expenses | 50% |
| Exhibitions, Trade Missions | 50% |
| Reinvestment Allowance | Up to 50% |

### Choice of Tax Regime

Registered Business Enterprises can choose between:
1. **SCIT:** 5% on gross income earned
2. **EDR:** 20% CIT on taxable income with additional deductions

These regimes cannot be availed simultaneously.

### Net Operating Loss Carry-Over (NOLCO)

Under CREATE MORE:
- Net operating losses during the first three years from commercial operations
- May be carried over as deductions from gross income
- Carry-over period: Five consecutive taxable years following the ITH period

## 8. Sunset Provisions for Existing Incentives

### Transition Rules for Pre-CREATE Registered Enterprises

| Scenario | Transition |
|----------|------------|
| ITH Only | Continue enjoying ITH for remaining period |
| ITH followed by 5% GIT | May enjoy 5% GIT for 10 years after ITH expires |
| Already enjoying 5% GIT | Continue for 10 years from CREATE effectivity |

### Sunset Periods Based on Previous GIE Duration

| Previous GIE Benefit Duration | Sunset Period |
|------------------------------|---------------|
| 10 years or more | 4 years |
| 5-10 years | 5 years |
| Less than 5 years | 7 years |
| Export with 10,000+ employees | 9 years |

### Re-application Provisions

After the transitory period expires:
- Export enterprises may reapply for 5% SCIT for 10 years
- Subject to SIPP conditions and FIRB performance review
- **Important:** Companies can only register again for a NEW activity entitled to incentives

### CREATE MORE Updates

- Pre-CREATE RBEs have until **December 31, 2034** to fully transition
- May continue to avail duty and VAT incentives even after transitory period
