# Philippine TRAIN Law (RA 10963)

## Executive Summary

Republic Act No. 10963, known as the Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion (TRAIN) Law, represents the first package of the Philippine government's Comprehensive Tax Reform Program (CTRP). Signed into law by President Rodrigo Duterte on December 19, 2017, and effective January 1, 2018, this landmark legislation fundamentally restructured the Philippine tax system.

---

## 1. Overview and Objectives of RA 10963

### Primary Objectives

1. **Tax System Simplification**: Reduce complexity across personal income tax, transfer taxes, and consumption taxes
2. **Revenue Generation**: Fund the Build Build Build infrastructure program and social services
3. **Equity Enhancement**: Provide income tax relief to 99% of individual taxpayers
4. **Economic Stimulus**: Increase consumer purchasing power
5. **Poverty Reduction**: Implement targeted cash transfers to poorest households

### Revenue Impact

TRAIN contributed PHP 575.8 billion in net additional revenues from 2018 to 2021.

## 2. Personal Income Tax Rate Changes (2018-2023 Schedule)

### Tax Schedule 1: January 1, 2018 to December 31, 2022

| Annual Taxable Income | Tax Rate/Amount |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| PHP 250,000 and below | 0% (Exempt) |
| Over PHP 250,000 to PHP 400,000 | 20% of excess over PHP 250,000 |
| Over PHP 400,000 to PHP 800,000 | PHP 30,000 + 25% of excess over PHP 400,000 |
| Over PHP 800,000 to PHP 2,000,000 | PHP 130,000 + 30% of excess over PHP 800,000 |
| Over PHP 2,000,000 to PHP 8,000,000 | PHP 490,000 + 32% of excess over PHP 2,000,000 |
| Over PHP 8,000,000 | PHP 2,410,000 + 35% of excess over PHP 8,000,000 |

### Tax Schedule 2: January 1, 2023 Onwards

| Annual Taxable Income | Tax Rate/Amount |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| PHP 250,000 and below | 0% (Exempt) |
| Over PHP 250,000 to PHP 400,000 | 15% of excess over PHP 250,000 |
| Over PHP 400,000 to PHP 800,000 | PHP 22,500 + 20% of excess over PHP 400,000 |
| Over PHP 800,000 to PHP 2,000,000 | PHP 102,500 + 25% of excess over PHP 800,000 |
| Over PHP 2,000,000 to PHP 8,000,000 | PHP 402,500 + 30% of excess over PHP 2,000,000 |
| Over PHP 8,000,000 | PHP 2,202,500 + 35% of excess over PHP 8,000,000 |

### Option for Self-Employed and Professionals

Self-employed individuals and professionals with gross sales/receipts not exceeding PHP 3 million annually may elect:
1. Graduated income tax rates, OR
2. **8% flat tax** on gross sales/receipts exceeding PHP 250,000

## 3. Estate and Donor's Tax Simplification

### Estate Tax Changes

| Feature | Pre-TRAIN | Under TRAIN |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Tax Rate | 5% to 20% (graduated) | **6% flat** |
| Standard Deduction | PHP 1,000,000 | **PHP 5,000,000** |
| Family Home | PHP 1,000,000 | **PHP 10,000,000** |
| Funeral Expenses | Up to PHP 200,000 | Removed |
| Medical Expenses | Up to PHP 500,000 | Removed |
| Filing Period | 6 months from death | **1 year from death** |

### Donor's Tax Changes

| Feature | Pre-TRAIN | Under TRAIN |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Tax Rate (Relatives) | 2% to 15% | **6% flat** |
| Tax Rate (Strangers) | 30% | **6% flat** |
| Exemption | PHP 100,000 | **PHP 250,000/year** |

| Net Selling Price | Tax Rate |
|-------------------|----------|
| Up to PHP 600,000 | 4% |

## 5. VAT Exemption Threshold Changes

### VAT Registration Threshold

**Maintained at PHP 3,000,000**: Businesses with annual gross sales of PHP 3 million and below remain VAT-exempt.

### Changes to VAT Exemptions

The TRAIN Law **repealed 54 provisions** related to VAT exemption to broaden the tax base.

**Retained Exemptions:**
- Senior citizens (qualified purchases)
- Persons with disabilities
- Cooperatives (tightened definitions)
- Residential rent (monthly rental not exceeding PHP 15,000)
- Agricultural products in original state
- Educational services

**New VAT Exemptions (Effective 2019):**
- Medicines for diabetes, cholesterol, and hypertension

---

## 6. Removal of Personal Exemptions

### Previous System (Pre-TRAIN)

| Exemption Type | Amount |
|----------------|--------|
| Basic Personal Exemption | PHP 50,000 |
| Additional Exemption per Dependent | PHP 25,000 (max 4) |
| Maximum Total Exemption | PHP 150,000 |

### TRAIN Law Changes (Effective January 1, 2018)

**Complete Removal**: Personal and additional exemptions were abolished entirely.

**Replacement**: The PHP 250,000 tax-free threshold for all individual taxpayers.

## 7. 13th Month and Bonus Exemption Increase

### Tax-Exempt Threshold

| Period | Exemption Limit |
|--------|-----------------|
| Pre-TRAIN (RA 10653) | PHP 82,000 |
| Under TRAIN | **PHP 90,000** |

### Coverage

The PHP 90,000 exemption applies to:
- 13th month pay
- Christmas bonus
- Productivity incentives
- Performance bonuses
- Other employer incentives
- Excess de minimis benefits

---

## 8. Fringe Benefits Tax Changes

### Tax Rate Adjustment

| Period | FBT Rate |
|--------|----------|
| Pre-TRAIN | 32% |
| Under TRAIN | **35%** |

### Liability

The employer shoulders the FBT; it is not deducted from the employee's benefit amount.

### Exemptions from FBT

1. Benefits required by the nature of business
2. Benefits exempted under special laws
3. Retirement, insurance, hospitalization contributions
4. Benefits to rank-and-file employees
5. Benefits under collective bargaining agreements

## Documentary Stamp Tax Changes

### General Rate Increase

Most DST rates were **doubled** (100% increase).

### Specific Rate Changes

| Document Type | Pre-TRAIN | TRAIN Rate |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| Original issue of shares | PHP 1.00/PHP 200 | PHP 2.00/PHP 200 |
| Transfer of shares | PHP 0.75/PHP 200 | PHP 1.50/PHP 200 |
| Debt instruments | PHP 1.00/PHP 200 | PHP 1.50/PHP 200 |
| Real property transfers | PHP 7.50/PHP 1,000 | PHP 15.00/PHP 1,000 |
| Proxies | PHP 15.00 | PHP 30.00 |
| Powers of attorney | PHP 5.00 | PHP 10.00 |
| Mortgages (up to PHP 5,000) | PHP 20.00 | PHP 40.00 |

### New DST Imposition

**Donation of Real Property**: PHP 15.00 per PHP 1,000 of value (1.5%)

## Constitutional Challenge

On January 24, 2023, the Supreme Court (en banc) **dismissed** consolidated petitions challenging the constitutionality of the TRAIN Law with a **13-1 vote**.
