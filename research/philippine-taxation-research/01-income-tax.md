# Philippine Income Tax Laws (2024-2026)

## Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of Philippine income tax laws covering individual and corporate taxation under the Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion (TRAIN) Law (Republic Act No. 10963), the Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises (CREATE) Act (Republic Act No. 11534), and the CREATE MORE Act (Republic Act No. 12066).

---

## 1. Individual Income Tax Rates and Brackets

### Legal Basis
- **TRAIN Law (RA 10963)** - Effective January 1, 2018, with phased implementation
- Current rates effective from **January 1, 2023 onwards**

### Income Tax Table (2023-2026)

| Annual Taxable Income | Tax Due |
|----------------------|---------|
| PHP 250,000 and below | **0% (Exempt)** |
| PHP 250,001 to PHP 400,000 | **15%** of the excess over PHP 250,000 |
| PHP 400,001 to PHP 800,000 | **PHP 22,500 + 20%** of the excess over PHP 400,000 |
| PHP 800,001 to PHP 2,000,000 | **PHP 102,500 + 25%** of the excess over PHP 800,000 |
| PHP 2,000,001 to PHP 8,000,000 | **PHP 402,500 + 30%** of the excess over PHP 2,000,000 |
| Over PHP 8,000,000 | **PHP 2,202,500 + 35%** of the excess over PHP 8,000,000 |

### Key Individual Tax Provisions

**Tax Exemptions:**
- Individuals earning **PHP 250,000 or less annually** are exempt from income tax
- **Minimum wage earners** are fully exempt under Section 22(H) of the Tax Code
- Personal and additional exemptions have been **abolished** under TRAIN Law

**8% Flat Tax Option:**
- Available to self-employed individuals and professionals
- Applies if gross sales/receipts do not exceed **PHP 3,000,000**
- Rate: **8%** of gross sales/receipts exceeding PHP 250,000
- In lieu of graduated income tax rates and percentage tax

## 2. Corporate Income Tax Rates

### Legal Basis
- **CREATE Act (RA 11534)** - Effective April 11, 2021
- **CREATE MORE Act (RA 12066)** - Effective November 28, 2024

### Regular Corporate Income Tax (RCIT)

| Corporation Type | Tax Rate | Basis |
|-----------------|----------|-------|
| **Domestic Corporations (Standard)** | 25% | Net taxable income |
| **MSMEs (Qualified Small Corporations)** | 20% | Net taxable income |
| **Resident Foreign Corporations** | 25% | Philippine-source income |
| **Non-Resident Foreign Corporations** | 25% | Gross income from Philippine sources |

### MSME Qualification Criteria
To qualify for the reduced **20% rate**, a corporation must meet **BOTH** conditions:
1. Net taxable income not exceeding **PHP 5,000,000**
2. Total assets not exceeding **PHP 100,000,000** (excluding land)

### Minimum Corporate Income Tax (MCIT)

| Provision | Rate/Requirement |
|-----------|------------------|
| MCIT Rate | **2%** of gross income |
| Applicability | Begins on the **4th taxable year** of operations |
| Rule | Pay whichever is higher: RCIT or MCIT |

### Branch Profit Remittance Tax (BPRT)
- **15%** on after-tax profits remitted by Philippine branches to foreign head offices
- May be reduced under applicable Double Taxation Agreements (DTAs)

### Dividends to Non-Resident Foreign Corporations
- Standard rate: **25%** final withholding tax
- Reduced rate: **15%** if the foreign country allows tax sparing credit

### CREATE MORE Act - Registered Business Enterprises (RBEs)

**Tax Regime Options:**

| Regime | Tax Rate | Basis | Duration |
|--------|----------|-------|----------|
| **Special Corporate Income Tax (SCIT)** | 5% | Gross income | Up to 17-27 years |
| **Enhanced Deductions Regime (EDR)** | 20% | Net taxable income | Up to 17-27 years |
| **Income Tax Holiday (ITH)** | 0% | N/A | 4-7 years |

## 3. Taxable vs. Non-Taxable Income

### Taxable Income (Section 32 of the NIRC)

**For Individuals:**
- Compensation income (salaries, wages, bonuses beyond exempt threshold)
- Business and professional income
- Passive income (interest, dividends, royalties, prizes)
- Capital gains
- Rental income
- Annuities and pensions (except from approved plans)

**For Corporations:**
- Gross income from operations
- Interest and dividend income
- Royalties and rental income
- Capital gains
- Other income from whatever source

### Non-Taxable Income

**A. 13th Month Pay and Other Benefits**

| Item | Tax-Exempt Ceiling |
|------|-------------------|
| 13th Month Pay and Other Benefits | **PHP 90,000** per year |
| Coverage | 13th month, 14th month, Christmas bonus, performance bonus, health bonus, perfect attendance bonus |
| Excess Treatment | Taxable as compensation income |

**B. De Minimis Benefits (Updated per RR 11-2018 and RR 4-2025)**

| Benefit Type | Tax-Exempt Limit |
|--------------|------------------|
| Monetized unused vacation leave | Up to 10 days (private employees) |
| Medical cash allowance | PHP 1,500/semester or PHP 250/month |
| Rice subsidy | PHP 2,000/month or 50kg rice |
| Uniform and clothing allowance | **PHP 7,000/month** (increased from PHP 6,000 in 2025) |
| Laundry allowance | PHP 300/month |
| Achievement awards | **PHP 10,000/year** |
| Christmas gifts | PHP 5,000/year |
| Daily meal allowance | Not exceeding 25% of basic minimum wage |

**C. Separation Pay (Section 32(B)(6)(b) of the Tax Code)**

| Separation Cause | Tax Treatment |
|------------------|---------------|
| Death | **Exempt** |
| Sickness | **Exempt** |
| Physical disability | **Exempt** |
| Retrenchment | **Exempt** |
| Redundancy | **Exempt** |
| Closure of business | **Exempt** |
| Voluntary resignation | **Taxable** |

## 4. Allowable Deductions

### Two Methods of Claiming Deductions

| Method | Individual Rate | Corporate Rate | Basis |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|-------|
| **Optional Standard Deduction (OSD)** | 40% | 40% | Gross sales/receipts (individuals); Gross income (corporations) |
| **Itemized Deductions** | Actual expenses | Actual expenses | Documented ordinary and necessary expenses |

### Itemized Deductions (Section 34 of the NIRC)

**A. Ordinary and Necessary Business Expenses (Section 34(A))**
- Salaries, wages, and compensation
- Rentals
- Utilities (electricity, water, communications)
- Advertising and promotional expenses
- Travel expenses
- Entertainment, amusement, and recreation (EAR) - limited to 0.5% of net sales for sellers of goods; 1% of net revenues for sellers of services

**B. Interest Expenses (Section 34(B))**
- Interest on business-related loans
- **Limitation:** Reduced by 20% of interest income subject to final tax

**C. Taxes (Section 34(C))**
- Local business taxes
- Real property taxes
- Documentary stamp taxes
- **Exclusion:** Income tax is NOT deductible

**D. Losses (Section 34(D))**
- Casualty losses (theft, fire, storm, calamity)
- Net Operating Loss Carry-Over (NOLCO) - 3 years for regular taxpayers; 5 years under special provisions

**E. Bad Debts (Section 34(E))**
- Must be previously included in gross income
- Reasonable collection efforts required

**F. Depreciation (Section 34(F))**
- Reasonable allowance for exhaustion, wear and tear, obsolescence

**G. Charitable Contributions (Section 34(H))**

| Donee Type | Deductible Limit |
|------------|------------------|
| Government entities | Up to 5% of taxable income |
| Accredited NGOs/charitable organizations | Up to 10% of taxable income |
| Priority activities (education, health, etc.) | Full deduction |

### Requirements for Deductibility (4 BIR Rules)
1. **Ordinary and necessary** for the business
2. **Paid or incurred** within the taxable year
3. **Directly related** to earning income
4. **Properly documented** with official receipts

## 5. Filing Requirements and Deadlines

### Key Income Tax Return Forms

| Form Number | Description | Who Files |
|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| **BIR Form 1700** | Annual ITR for purely compensation income | Employees with one employer and no other income |
| **BIR Form 1701** | Annual ITR for mixed income/self-employed | Self-employed, professionals, mixed-income earners |
| **BIR Form 1701A** | Annual ITR for 8% flat tax option | Self-employed/professionals under 8% regime |
| **BIR Form 1701Q** | Quarterly ITR | Self-employed and professionals |
| **BIR Form 1702** | Annual ITR for corporations | Domestic and resident foreign corporations |
| **BIR Form 1702Q** | Quarterly ITR for corporations | All corporations |
| **BIR Form 2316** | Certificate of Compensation Payment/Tax Withheld | Issued by employers to employees |

### Key Deadlines for Calendar Year Taxpayers

| Filing Requirement | Deadline |
|--------------------|----------|
| **Annual ITR (Forms 1700, 1701, 1702)** | **April 15** of the following year |
| **Quarterly ITR (1st Quarter)** | May 15 |
| **Quarterly ITR (2nd Quarter)** | August 15 |
| **Quarterly ITR (3rd Quarter)** | November 15 |
| **BIR Form 2316 distribution to employees** | **January 31** of the following year |

## 6. Penalties for Non-Compliance

### Civil Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| **Late Filing** | **25% surcharge** on tax due |
| **Late Payment** | **20% interest per annum** on unpaid tax |
| **Substantial Understatement** | **50% surcharge** (fraud/willful neglect) |
| **Deficiency Tax** | 25% surcharge + 20% interest |

### Criminal Penalties (Section 255 of the NIRC)

| Offense | Penalty |
|---------|---------|
| **Willful failure to file return/pay tax** | Fine: Not less than **PHP 10,000**; Imprisonment: **1-10 years** |
| **Tax evasion** | Fine + Imprisonment: **6-10 years** |
