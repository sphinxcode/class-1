# Philippine Tax Administration and Compliance

## Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of Philippine tax administration and compliance under the National Internal Revenue Code (NIRC), covering the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) structure, registration requirements, electronic filing systems, tax forms, audit procedures, taxpayer rights and remedies, appeal mechanisms, Court of Tax Appeals jurisdiction, compromise programs, and statute of limitations provisions.

---

## 1. Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) Structure and Powers

### Overview

The Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) is the primary revenue service for the Philippine government, collecting more than half of the total tax revenues. The BIR operates under the Department of Finance.

### Powers and Duties

- **Assessment and Collection**: All national internal revenue taxes, fees, and charges
- **Enforcement**: Forfeitures, penalties, and fines connected with tax violations
- **Execution of Judgments**: Cases decided in its favor by CTA and ordinary courts
- **Supervisory Powers**: Administration of supervisory and police powers

### Organizational Structure

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Commissioner's Office** | Overall leadership and policy direction |
| **Deputy Commissioners** | Oversee major operational groups |
| **Regional Offices (ROs)** | Implement national policies |
| **Revenue District Offices (RDOs)** | Front-line tax administration |
| **Revenue Data Centers (RDCs)** | Manage information systems |

## 2. Tax Registration Requirements (TIN and COR)

### Who Must Register

1. **Sole Proprietors** - Individuals conducting trade or business
2. **Professionals** - Accountants, doctors, lawyers, consultants
3. **Partnerships and Corporations** - SEC-registered entities
4. **Cooperatives and Other Entities**
5. **Non-Stock, Non-Profit Organizations**

### Certificate of Registration (COR/Form 2303)

**Contents of COR:**
- Unique Tax Identification Number (TIN)
- Specific taxes the business must pay
- Business name and address
- Line of business or profession

### Documentary Requirements

**For Corporations/Partnerships (BIR Form 1903):**
- SEC Certificate of Registration
- Articles of Incorporation and By-laws
- Mayor's Permit
- Barangay Clearance
- Lease contract or proof of business address
- Valid government-issued IDs of officers

**For Individuals/Self-Employed (BIR Form 1901):**
- Valid government-issued ID
- Birth Certificate or passport
- Barangay Clearance
- Mayor's Permit
- Lease contract

### Registration Fees

| Fee Type | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Registration Fee (BIR Form 0605) | PHP 500 |
| Documentary Stamp Tax | PHP 30 |

## 5. Tax Audit Procedures and Letter of Authority

### Legal Basis for Tax Audits

- **Section 5, NIRC**: Commissioner power to assess and collect taxes
- **Section 6(A), NIRC**: Authorizes examination of taxpayer records
- **Section 13, NIRC**: Mandates LOA for revenue officers

### Letter of Authority (LOA) Requirements

**Essential Elements of a Valid LOA:**

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Taxpayer Identification | Name and TIN |
| Taxable Period | Specific year or period covered |
| Tax Types | Types of taxes covered |
| Revenue Officers | Names and signatures |
| Approving Authority | Signature of appropriate official |
| No Alterations | No handwritten changes |

### LOA Timelines

| Stage | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Commencement of Examination | Within 10 days from service |
| Completion of Audit | 120 days from receipt of LOA |
| RDO Cases | 180 days total |
| Large Taxpayer Cases | 240 days total |

### Tax Audit Process Flow

```
LOA Issued → LOA Served → Examination → Notice of Discrepancy (NOD)
     ↓ (30 days to respond)
Preliminary Assessment Notice (PAN)
     ↓ (15 days to respond)
Final Assessment Notice/Formal Letter of Demand (FAN/FLD)
     ↓ (30 days to protest)
Taxpayer Protest
```

## 6. Taxpayer Rights and Remedies

### Administrative Remedies

**Request for Reconsideration:**
- Review based on errors in law or findings of fact
- Filed within 30 days from receipt of FAN/FLD

**Request for Reinvestigation:**
- Presentation of additional evidence
- Filed within 30 days from receipt of FAN/FLD
- Additional 60 days to submit supporting documents

### Judicial Remedies

| Remedy | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Appeal to CTA Division | Within 30 days from final decision |
| Appeal to CTA En Banc | From CTA Division decision |
| Appeal to Supreme Court | Verified petition for certiorari |

## 7. Protest and Appeal Procedures

### Filing Administrative Protest

**Requirements for a Valid Protest:**
1. Filed within 30 days from receipt of FAN/FLD
2. Addressed to the Commissioner
3. Contains clear statement of grounds
4. Supported by documentary evidence

**Consequences of Invalid or Late Protest:**
- Assessment becomes final and executory
- No further administrative or judicial appeal available

### BIR Action on Protest

The BIR has **180 days** to act on the protest. If the BIR fails to act:

**Option 1**: Appeal to CTA within 30 days after the 180-day period expires

**Option 2**: Wait for the final decision and appeal within 30 days of receipt

## 8. Court of Tax Appeals Jurisdiction

### Composition

- One (1) Presiding Justice
- Eight (8) Associate Justices
- May sit en banc or in three divisions

### Exclusive Appellate Jurisdiction

| Subject Matter | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Disputed Assessments** | Decisions of CIR on tax assessments |
| **Tax Refunds** | Claims for refund |
| **Penalties** | Penalties under NIRC |
| **Customs Cases** | Commissioner of Customs decisions |
| **Local Tax Cases** | RTC decisions in local tax matters |

### Exclusive Original Jurisdiction

| Subject Matter | Threshold |
|----------------|-----------|
| **Tax Collection Cases** | Amount exceeding PHP 1,000,000 |
| **Criminal Tax Offenses** | NIRC and CMTA violations |

## 9. Compromise and Abatement Programs

### Compromise Settlement (Section 204, NIRC)

**Ground 1: Doubtful Validity**
- Minimum Compromise Rate: 40% of basic assessed tax

**Ground 2: Financial Incapacity**

| Condition | Compromise Rate |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Low/no-income individuals | 10% |
| Zero or negative net worth | 10% |
| Dissolved corporations | 20% |
| Bankrupt/insolvent taxpayers | 20% |
| Severe capital impairment | 40% |

### What Cannot Be Compromised

- Criminal violations already filed in court
- Cases involving fraud

### Abatement of Penalties (Section 204, NIRC)

**Grounds for Abatement:**

| Ground | Applicable To |
|--------|---------------|
| Filing/payment in wrong venue | Surcharge, interest, penalty |
| Mistake in payment | Surcharge, interest, penalty |
| Difficult interpretation of law | Surcharge, interest, penalty |
| One day late due to bank cut-off | Surcharge, penalty |
| Wrong tax form but correct amount | Surcharge, penalty |

## 10. Running of Statute of Limitations

### Prescriptive Period for Assessment

| Circumstance | Period | Starting Point |
|--------------|--------|----------------|
| **Ordinary Assessment** | 3 years | Later of: filing deadline or actual filing |
| **Extraordinary Assessment** | 10 years | Discovery of false/fraudulent return |

### Prescriptive Period for Collection

| Type of Assessment | Collection Period |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| Assessment within 3-year period | 3 years from assessment |
| Assessment within 10-year period | 5 years from assessment |
| Assessment after waiver extension | 5 years from assessment |

### Grounds for Suspension

| Ground | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Waiver of Statute of Limitations** | Period extended |
| **Request for Reinvestigation** | Suspended until complete |
| **Taxpayer Cannot Be Located** | Suspended |
| **Taxpayer Outside Philippines** | Suspended |
| **Pending Administrative Protest** | Period suspended |
| **Pending Judicial Appeal** | Period suspended |

### Requirements for Valid Waiver

1. Proper form prescribed by RMO 20-90
2. Signed by taxpayer or authorized representative
3. For corporations, signed by responsible official
4. Delegation in writing and notarized
5. Signed by CIR or authorized revenue official
6. Date of BIR acceptance indicated
7. Both parties must agree in writing
