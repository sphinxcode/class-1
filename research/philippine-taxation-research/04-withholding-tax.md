# Philippine Withholding Tax Laws

## Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of Philippine withholding tax regulations as administered by the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR). The Philippines employs a robust withholding tax system covering compensation income, expanded/creditable withholding taxes, final withholding taxes on passive income, and special provisions for foreign corporations.

## 3. Withholding Tax on Compensation

### Tax-Exempt Threshold
- Annual taxable income up to **PHP 250,000** is exempt
- Monthly equivalent: approximately **PHP 20,833**

### Revised Withholding Tax Table (Effective January 1, 2023 onwards)

| Annual Taxable Income | Monthly Equivalent | Tax Computation |
|----------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Up to PHP 250,000 | Up to PHP 20,833 | 0% |
| PHP 250,001 - PHP 400,000 | PHP 20,833 - PHP 33,333 | PHP 0 + 15% of excess over PHP 250,000 |
| PHP 400,001 - PHP 800,000 | PHP 33,333 - PHP 66,667 | PHP 22,500 + 20% of excess over PHP 400,000 |
| PHP 800,001 - PHP 2,000,000 | PHP 66,667 - PHP 166,667 | PHP 102,500 + 25% of excess over PHP 800,000 |
| PHP 2,000,001 - PHP 8,000,000 | PHP 166,667 - PHP 666,667 | PHP 402,500 + 30% of excess over PHP 2,000,000 |
| Over PHP 8,000,000 | Over PHP 666,667 | PHP 2,202,500 + 35% of excess over PHP 8,000,000 |

### Key BIR Forms and Deadlines
- **BIR Form 1601-C**: Monthly remittance, due 10th of following month
- **BIR Form 2316**: Certificate of Compensation Payment/Tax Withheld, due January 31
- **BIR Form 1604-C**: Annual Information Return with Alphalist, due January 31

## 5. Foreign Corporation Withholding Rules

### Non-Resident Foreign Corporations (NRFC) - General Rules

| Income Type | Withholding Rate |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Gross income from Philippine sources | 25% |
| Dividends (general) | 25% |
| Dividends (with tax sparing credit) | 15% |
| Interest | 25% |
| Interest on foreign loans | 20% |
| Royalties | 25% |
| Service fees (cross-border) | 25% + 12% VAT |

### Cross-Border Service Payments
Per BIR rulings, outbound service payments to foreign providers are:
- Subject to **25% final withholding income tax**
- Subject to **12% final withholding VAT**

## 6. Tax Treaty Relief Provisions

### Overview
The Philippines maintains tax treaties with **44 countries** that provide preferential withholding tax rates.

### Common Treaty Rates

| Income Type | Typical Treaty Rate | Standard Rate |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Dividends (substantial ownership) | 10-15% | 25% |
| Dividends (portfolio) | 15-25% | 25% |
| Interest | 10-15% | 20-25% |
| Royalties (IP, patents) | 10-15% | 25% |

### Tax Treaty Relief Application (TTRA) Procedure

#### Types of Applications

**1. Request for Confirmation (RFC)**
- Filed when withholding agent applies treaty rate outright
- Filed not later than the last day of the fourth month following close of taxable year

**2. Tax Treaty Relief Application (TTRA)**
- Filed when regular rate was applied
- Non-resident seeks refund/credit

#### Documentary Requirements
- BIR Form 0901 (Application Form)
- Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) from foreign tax authority
- Relevant tax treaty provisions
- Notarized Special Power of Attorney (for representatives)
