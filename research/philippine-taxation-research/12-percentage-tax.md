# Philippine Percentage Tax Laws

## Executive Summary

Philippine Percentage Taxes are business-style taxes imposed under Title V of the National Internal Revenue Code (NIRC), as amended. Unlike Value-Added Tax (VAT), percentage taxes are levied on gross sales, receipts, or other statutory bases of specific businesses.

---

## 1. Non-VAT Registered Persons (Section 116)

**Tax Rate: 3% of quarterly gross sales**

Persons whose gross annual sales or receipts do not exceed PHP 3,000,000 (the VAT threshold) and are not VAT-registered are subject to percentage tax.

**Key Points:**
- Tax base changed from "gross receipts" to "gross sales" under EOPT Act (RA 11976)
- Temporary reduction to **1%** was in effect from July 1, 2020 to June 30, 2023 under CREATE Law
- Rate has reverted to **3%** after June 30, 2023
- Self-employed may opt for **8% income tax** in lieu of graduated rates and 3% percentage tax

---

## 2. Domestic Carriers and Keepers of Garages (Section 117)

**Tax Rate: 3% of quarterly gross sales**

**Covered Entities:**
- Cars for rent or hire driven by the lessee
- Transportation contractors
- Persons who transport passengers for hire
- Other domestic carriers by land

**Exemptions:**
- Owners of bancas
- Owners of animal-drawn two-wheeled vehicles

---

## 3. International Carriers Tax (Section 118)

**Tax Rate: 3% of quarterly gross sales** (on cargo transport)

| Type of Carrier | Tax Rate | Tax Base |
|-----------------|----------|----------|
| International Air Carriers (cargo) | 3% | Quarterly gross sales from cargo transport |
| International Shipping Carriers (cargo) | 3% | Quarterly gross sales from cargo transport |

**Additional Tax - Gross Philippine Billings:**
- **2.5% income tax** on Gross Philippine Billings

---

## 4. Franchise Tax (Section 119)

| Type of Franchise | Tax Rate | Tax Base |
|-------------------|----------|----------|
| Radio/TV Broadcasting (≤ PHP 10 million) | 3% | Gross receipts |
| Gas Utilities | 2% | Gross receipts |
| Water Utilities | 2% | Gross receipts |

**Key Changes:**
- Electric utilities now subject to **12% VAT** under RA 9337
- Radio/TV broadcasting can opt for VAT registration (irrevocable)

---

## 5. Overseas Dispatch/Communication Companies (Section 120)

**Tax Rate: 10% on the amount paid for services**

**Covered Services:**
- Telephone
- Telegraph
- Telewriter exchange
- Wireless communications

**Exemptions:**
1. Diplomatic services
2. International organizations
3. News services
4. Government communications

---

## 6. Banks and Non-Bank Financial Intermediaries (Sections 121 & 122)

### Section 121: Banks and NBFIs with Quasi-Banking Functions

| Type of Income | Tax Rate |
|----------------|----------|
| Interest, commissions (maturity ≤ 5 years) | 5% |
| Interest, commissions (maturity > 5 years) | 1% |
| Dividends and equity shares in subsidiaries | 0% |
| Other items (royalties, rentals, trading gains) | 7% |

### Section 122: Other Non-Bank Financial Intermediaries

| Type of Income | Tax Rate |
|----------------|----------|
| Interest, commissions (maturity ≤ 5 years) | 5% |
| Interest, commissions (maturity > 5 years) | 1% |
| All other items | 5% |

**Examples:** Pawnshops, money changers, finance companies

---

## 7. Life Insurance Companies (Section 123)

**Tax Rate: 2% of total premiums collected**

Originally **5%**, reduced to **2%** by Republic Act No. 10001.

**Covered:**
- All persons doing life insurance business in the Philippines
- Health and accident insurance premiums

**Variable Unit-Linked (VUL) Policies:** The 2% tax applies only to the insurance portion

---

## 8. Agents of Foreign Insurance Companies (Section 124)

| Category | Tax Rate | Tax Base |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Authorized agents for foreign companies | 4% | Total premiums collected |
| Property owners obtaining direct insurance | 5% | Total premiums paid |

**Note:** Reinsurance is exempt from this tax.

---

## 9. Amusement Taxes (Section 125)

| Type of Amusement | Tax Rate |
|-------------------|----------|
| Cockpits | 18% |
| Cabarets, Night or Day Clubs | 18% |
| Boxing Exhibitions | 10% |
| Professional Basketball Games | 15% |
| Jai-Alai | 30% |
| Racetracks | 30% |

**Special Exemption for Boxing:**
World or Oriental Championship boxing exhibitions are exempt if at least one contender is Filipino.

**Tax Base:** Gross receipts (includes TV, radio, and motion picture rights)

---

## 10. Winnings from Horse Racing (Section 126)

| Type of Winnings | Tax Rate |
|------------------|----------|
| Regular winnings/dividends | 10% |
| Double, Forecast/Quinella, Trifecta bets | 4% |
| Owners of winning race horses (prizes) | 10% |

---

## 11. Stock Transaction Tax (Section 127)

### Section 127(A) - Listed Stock Exchanges

| Period | Tax Rate | Tax Base |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Prior to TRAIN Law | 0.5% | Gross selling price |
| Under TRAIN Law (2018) | 0.6% | Gross selling price |
| Effective July 1, 2025 (CMEPA) | **0.1%** | Gross selling price |

**Current Rate (as of July 2025): 0.1%**

### Section 127(B) - Initial Public Offering (IPO) Tax - REPEALED

Repealed by Republic Act No. 11494 (Bayanihan to Recover as One Act), effective September 15, 2020.

## Recent Legislative Changes

1. **Ease of Paying Taxes Act (RA 11976, 2024):** Changed tax base from "gross receipts" to "gross sales" for Sections 116, 117, and 118

2. **CREATE Law (RA 11534, 2021):** Temporarily reduced percentage tax under Section 116 from 3% to 1% (July 1, 2020 to June 30, 2023)

3. **Capital Markets Efficiency Promotion Act (CMEPA, 2025):** Reduced stock transaction tax from 0.6% to 0.1% effective July 1, 2025

4. **Bayanihan to Recover as One Act (RA 11494, 2020):** Repealed the IPO tax under Section 127(B)
