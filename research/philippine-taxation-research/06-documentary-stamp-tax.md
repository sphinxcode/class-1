# Philippine Documentary Stamp Tax (DST)

## Executive Summary

Documentary Stamp Tax (DST) is an excise tax imposed under Title VII (Sections 173-201) of the National Internal Revenue Code (NIRC) of 1997, as amended. The tax applies to documents, instruments, loan agreements, and papers that evidence the creation, transfer, or acceptance of rights, obligations, or properties.

---

## 1. Documents Subject to DST

### Stock and Securities Transactions
- Original issuance of shares of stock (Section 174)
- Sales, agreements to sell, transfers of shares/stock certificates (Section 175)
- Bonds, debentures, certificates of indebtedness (Section 176)
- Certificates of profits or interest in property (Section 177)

### Banking and Financial Instruments
- Bank checks, drafts, certificates of deposit (Section 178)
- All debt instruments including loan agreements, promissory notes (Section 179)
- Bills of exchange or drafts (Section 180)
- Acceptance of bills of exchange (Section 181)
- Foreign bills of exchange and letters of credit (Section 182)

### Insurance Documents
- Life insurance policies (Section 183)
- Policies of insurance upon property (Section 184)
- Fidelity bonds and other insurance policies (Section 185)
- Policies of annuities and pre-need plans (Section 186)
- Indemnity bonds (Section 187)

### Miscellaneous Documents
- Certificates of various types (Section 188)
- Warehouse receipts (Section 189)
- Leases and other hiring agreements (Section 194)
- Mortgages, pledges, and deeds of trust (Section 195)
- Deeds of sale and conveyances of real property (Section 196)

## 3. Exempt Documents and Transactions (Section 199)

### Insurance-Related Exemptions
- Policies by fraternal or beneficiary societies for sole benefit of members

### Government-Related Exemptions
- Certificates by government officials in official capacity
- Instruments issued by the Republic of the Philippines, its agencies, and LGUs
- Loans or agreements issued by government

### Securities and Financial Market Exemptions
- Securities borrowing and lending transactions
- Sale of shares listed and traded through local stock exchanges
- Secondary market trading of fixed income securities
- Derivatives, including repurchase agreements
- Interbranch advances within the same legal entity
- Bank deposits without fixed terms
- Interbank call loans (maturity not exceeding 7 days)

### Personal and Small Transaction Exemptions
- Loan agreements not exceeding P250,000 by individuals for personal purchases

### New Exemptions Under CMEPA (Effective July 1, 2025)
- Sale of shares listed and traded through local or foreign stock exchanges
- Original issuance of shares in mutual fund companies
- Issuance of certificates in mutual funds or UITFs

## 4. Who is Liable for DST Payment

### General Rule (Section 173)
The DST shall be paid by the person **making, signing, issuing, accepting, or transferring** the document.

### Exception - Exempt Party
When one party to the taxable document is exempt, the **other party who is not exempt** shall be directly liable.

### Practical Application

| Transaction Type | Typically Liable Party |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| Loan agreements | Borrower |
| Sale of real property | Buyer |
| Promissory notes | Maker/Issuer |
| Stock issuance | Issuing corporation |
| Insurance policies | Insurance company |

## 5. Filing and Payment Procedures

### BIR Forms Used
- **BIR Form 2000**: Documentary Stamp Tax Declaration Return (monthly)
- **BIR Form 2000-OT**: For one-time transactions

### Filing Deadline
Within **five (5) days after the close of the month** when the taxable document was made.

### Where to File
1. Authorized Agent Bank (AAB) within the RDO's jurisdiction
2. Revenue Collection Officer if no AAB available

### Payment Methods
- Authorized Agent Bank (AAB)
- GCash
- LandBank LBP-Link.biz portal
- eBIRForms / eDST system

## 6. Penalties for Non-Payment or Late Payment

### Civil Penalties

| Penalty Type | Rate |
|--------------|------|
| **Surcharge** | 25% of basic tax due |
| **Surcharge (Fraud)** | 50% of basic tax due |
| **Interest** | 12% per annum |
| **Compromise Penalty** | P200 to P50,000 |

### Criminal Sanctions (Section 255 of NIRC)
- **Willful evasion**: Fines up to P100,000 and/or imprisonment

### Practical Consequences of Non-Payment
- Documents may be **refused for recording/registration**
- Issues on **admissibility** as evidence until proper DST is paid

## 7. Recent Amendments

### TRAIN Law (RA 10963) - Effective January 1, 2018

Most DST rates were **doubled** (100% increase):
- Section 174 (Original shares): P1.00 to P2.00 per P200
- Section 175 (Share transfers): P0.75 to P1.50 per P200
- Section 178 (Bank checks): P1.50 to P3.00
- Section 188, 189, 192 (Certificates, receipts, proxies): P15.00 to P30.00
- Section 194 (Leases): P3.00 to P6.00 base rate
- Section 195 (Mortgages): P20.00 to P40.00 base rate

**New Imposition**: Donations of real property now taxed at 1.5%

### Capital Markets Efficiency Promotion Act (CMEPA/RA 12214) - Effective July 1, 2025

**Reduced DST on Original Share Issuances:**
- Previous: 1% of par value
- New: 0.75% of par value

**New DST Exemptions:**
- Sale of shares listed/traded on stock exchanges
- Original issuance/redemption of shares in mutual fund companies
- Issuance of participation certificates in mutual funds or UITFs
